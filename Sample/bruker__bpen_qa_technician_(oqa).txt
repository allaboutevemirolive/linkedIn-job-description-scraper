BPEN_QA Technician (OQA)

Overview




Maintains quality standards by approving incoming materials, in-process production, and finished products; recording quality results




Responsibilities




 * Performing quality inspection of products priore to inspection to customers.
 * Assisting or determining methods, sequences and procedures necessary for inspection.
 * Translating drawings and specifications into effective measurement plans.
 * Approves incoming materials and in-process production by confirming specifications; conducting visual and measurement tests; rejecting and returning unacceptable materials.
 * Approves in-process production by confirming system specifications, and quality plans; conducting visual and measurement tests; communicating required adjustments to production supervisor.
 * Approves finished products by confirming specifications; conducting visual and measurement tests; returning products for re-work; confirming re-work through BPEN process, i.e. NCMR, CAR, SCAR, etc.
 * Documents inspection results by completing reports and logs; summarizing re-work and waste; inputting data into quality database.
 * Keeps measurement equipment operating by following operating instructions; calling for repairs, certifications and recalibrations if required.
 * Maintains safe and healthy work environment by following standards and procedures; complying with legal regulations.
 * Effectively communicating with the Operations Team (i.e., Manufacturing Engineering, Supply Chain, Production, and Design Engineering) regarding drawings on technical issues.
 * Updates job knowledge by participating in educational opportunities; reading technical publications.
 * Accomplishes quality and organization mission by completing related results as needed.
 * Participating in and driving continuous improvement activities.
 * Other duties as assigned.




Qualifications
 * 
    * Minimum Diploma in any relevant field
    * Experience in IQA / OQA job functions - minimum 3-5 years of experience

 * Must be able to lift 50 pounds
 * Ability to use standard metrology tools (e.g., Caliper, Height Gauges, Micrometers, Plug and Tread Gauges, Optical Comparator, CMMs, cable continuity testers)
 * Familiarity with inspection of mechanical, electrical and optical components and assemblies
 * Able to read and understand complex engineering drawing tolerances and GDT, and translate requirements to parts/assemblies, including knowledge of electrical schematic drawings and wire diagrams.
 * Ability to use Microsoft Office products to facilitate data collection, analysis and reporting
 * Familiarity with standard manufacturing methods and procedures
 * Ability to read product drawings and translate product specifications into effective quality management plans
